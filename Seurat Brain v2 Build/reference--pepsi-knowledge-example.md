## Competitive Context
- **CSD:** Major competitive threat from energy drinks (Monster, Red Bull), functional water, and other beverages stealing share from soda over 30 years; Celsius as distribution partner also a competitor-to-watch
- **RTD Tea:** Pepsi/Unilever (PLP) holds ~50% share; threat from specialty (kombucha, matcha, yerba mate — all struggling as categories); hot tea separate business unit; DIY cold tea = ongoing innovation question
- **Water:** LIFEWTR losing to Essentia, Core, Topo Chico, Sparkling Ice; consumers lack education on LIFEWTR's "why"
- **Functional:** GLP-1 impact on protein; weight management evolution; gut health (kombucha); mental focus (Alpha Brain analogs); longevity trend; "food as medicine"
- **Snacking (value):** Value strategy work driven by inflationary context (2022); PepsiCo earnings growth was ~17% price-driven, not volume-driven
- **RTD Coffee (NACP):** Massive proliferation in coffee-forward space (cold brew, iced coffee, latte); rising competition from Pop & Bottle, Monster Java, Stok, La Colombe, Chobani, Califia; brand monogamy declining especially with younger (Gen Z) consumers; Frappuccino is the most mature / recognized brand but a difficult growth path; chilled multi-serve is nascent but growing fast
- **PC Snacking (Canada):** Functional popcorn emerging (e.g., Khloud protein popcorn); Daily Crunch (sprouted almonds in chip flavors) shows innovation direction; Liquid Death continues as Gen Z benchmark for disruptive joy

## Consumer & Shopper Behavior

### Beverages
- **Water:** Consumers driven by 5 macro need states (from 2019 framework); emotional + functional; fuzzy definition of "what is water" (is Sparkling Ice a water?); functional benefits are next wave but emotional experience also critical
- **RTD Tea:** Penetration + growth declining; premium tea (Pure Leaf) strong; specialty struggling; DIY cold tea at home = threat; tea needs to articulate "right to win" vs. entire LRB landscape; functional tea benefits (gut health, energy, cognitive) = emerging; food service lens important
- **CSD:** Share-of-stomach challenge; semiotic analysis needed to understand what adjacent categories are doing better; "leading-edge consumers" = important lens; innovation territories from qualitative → quantitative → sized
- **Functional (future-looking):** Fundamental needs don't change; what changes is what drives the need and what solutions people accept (e.g., weight management: no fat → no carbs → plant-based → GLP-1); three PEP functional priority spaces = energy, hydration, protein; emerging = mental focus, gut health, longevity; 2026 agenda also includes "social beverages" and "influencer markets" (Korea, boutique foodservice) as signal sources
- **RTD Coffee (NACP) — from Phase 1 qual research:**
  - Coffee occupies a "Jump Start" demand space (PepsiCo's framing): getting ready for the day, setting the course; more permissible than core energy
  - Coffee energy = more permissible than core or natural energy; perceived as natural (not artificial/scary); coffee taste is a positive cue
  - Morning is the primary occasion; afternoon coffee = more of a treat/incentive; café = social/experiential; RTD = convenience-driven
  - Key consumer dimensions surfacing as segmentation drivers: (1) nutrition philosophy / health mindfulness, (2) coffee involvement/knowledge, (3) life stage, (4) level of exploration, (5) strength of energy needed, (6) work-from-home vs. not, (7) routine vs. experiential orientation
  - Lack of perceived differentiation in cold coffee is extreme — everything feels interchangeable; brands need to stand for different things and pull subcategories apart
  - Cold brew and iced coffee conflated by most consumers; cold brew/nitro often grouped together despite meaningful differences
  - Health concern is a perennial challenge; perception that all RTD coffee is sugary (even among people who don't drink black coffee)
  - Alternative milks (oat, almond) are a meaningful barrier for some consumers; dairy alternatives not prominently featured in RTD
  - Café wins on experience and customization; RTD wins on convenience; opportunity to bring "café quality" cues to RTD
  - Multi-serve chilled perceived as offering value (25% of in-café prices) and "coffee your way" customization option
  - Pink drink and Paradise are perceived as juice (not coffee) by most; won't fit with core category; Gen Z shows some interest but they're outliers
  - Tripleshot = high caffeine / function-forward; Frappuccino = indulgent treat; Cold Brew = more permissible/straight-up; Nitro = small/strong/bitter
  - Protein coffees: protein is attractive concept but current executions disappoint on taste

- **RTD Coffee (NACP) — Phase 1 segmentation (consumer segments, aligned Oct–Nov 2023):**
  - NACP started with 8 segments in preliminary work; collapsed to **5 final segments** after pairs proved indistinguishable
  - Team aligned statement: "this segmentation has unique ways in for each segment" (Oct 16, 2023 touchbase)
  - **Segment 1 — Offbeat Optimists:** Confirmed name; paired with specific NACP brands; artificial barrier = **perceived (not specific) artificiality** — it is the overall sense of the product being artificial, not concern about specific ingredients; Starbucks brand brings some credibility but does not fully overcome this barrier
  - **Segment 2 — Contented Caregivers:** Confirmed name; highest home cold coffee engagement across all segments; driven by value + hot coffee affinity; home cold coffee behavior makes MS Chilled a natural fit
  - **Segment 3:** Extreme over-indexer; name not captured in documents read to date
  - **Segments 4–5:** Not yet named/confirmed in documents read to date
  - Segmentation built using **Q201/Q204** (coffee knowledge/behavior) as the primary definers; lifestyle Q102 used as secondary definer
  - 8→5 consolidation: several segment pairs proved indistinguishable from each other and were merged
  - Universal truths identified: desire to learn, family orientation, H&W attitudes
  - Starbucks brand promise aligns with "self-reliant/world-oriented" consumer values (noted in segmentation TB)
  - PepsiCo naming conventions: KST = key strategic target (demographic); CDNA = media target; design target = psychographic + demographic + aspirational but attainable ("aspirtainable")

- **RTD Coffee (NACP) — Jump Start micro-occasions (confirmed Oct 2023):**
  - Jump Start demand space sub-segmented into **5 micro-occasions**:
    1. **Coffee Comfort** — slow down, savor, mood-lift; RTD (Coffee Energy brand specifically) over-indexes here vs. overall RTD skew
    2. **Indulgent Pick-Me-Up** — sweet kick, craving, feeling motivated; indulgent treat driver
    3. **Routine Simplicity** — quickly taking caffeine at home; habitual/functional; minimal ceremony
    4. **Work Power Up** — task-focused, study-driven, concentration; function-forward
    5. **Coffee Commute** — in-car, convenient, craving-driven; commuting clusters emerged quickly in research
  - Commuting occasions clustered especially quickly during research sessions
  - **Micro-occasions = marketing targeting tools, not product slot assignments** — not every micro-occasion maps to a distinct product; these are lenses for activation, not a portfolio architecture mandate
  - Sizing was conducted at the **segment level** (not micro-occasion level)

- **RTD Coffee (NACP) — Phase 2 qual (BGT/BFY opportunities, Feb–Mar 2024):**
  - Phase 2 focused specifically on RTD Non-Triers — health-forward consumers who drink cold coffee at home or at café but have not engaged with RTD
  - Non-Trier profile: health-conscious, active, sugar-aware; mix of athletes, young professionals, college students, busy parents; water and home-brewed coffee are primary beverage staples; avoiding soda; Celsius seen as cautionary tale (tried/abandoned due to health concerns)
  - Core RTD barrier = **ingredient skepticism**: Non-Triers associate RTD with added sugars, artificial preservatives, and unrecognizable ingredients; believe RTD is "unhealthier than normal coffee"; trust of RTD brands is low even when café brand (Starbucks) is trusted
  - Frappuccino specific rejection: seen as "milk bottle from the 50s," "syrupy," "put on an ice cream" — associated with indulgence/dessert, not everyday coffee; Dunkin RTD = prototype of what to avoid
  - Cold Brew MS (Multi-Serve) is the most appealing RTD format for Non-Triers: offers perceived value, customization ("coffee your way"), and signals a more premium/serious coffee product
  - SS Iced Shaken Espresso = exciting because it mirrors café product consumers already love; credibility from familiarity with the café item
  - Ingredient transparency is the primary unlocking mechanism: RX Bar cited as benchmark; short/recognizable ingredient lists, no added sugars, no artificial preservatives = table stakes for conversion
  - Cold brew / iced coffee from La Colombe and Stok regarded as credible due to simpler ingredients vs. Starbucks RTD
  - Packaging cues matter: glass bottle > plastic (perceived quality); color of liquid visible = trust; can cueing energy drink = negative; design of new Cold Brew is visually appealing to this audience
  - "RTD is for people who know what they want and what they like" — Non-Triers feel overwhelmed by options and lack café-equivalent guidance; variety packs and in-store navigation aids (e.g., "if you like X in the café, try Y RTD") could drive trial
  - Shelving context: RTD shelved near energy drinks = negative association that reinforces stigma around sugar and artificiality
  - Pink & Paradise: Non-Triers generally not attracted (fruit, not coffee); occasionally some interest in lemonade variants if clearly labeled
  - Acute misbeliefs about RTD Coffee: consumers don't know what's inside products; opportunity for ingredient transparency to drive education (from Phase 2 debrief touchbase)
  - RTD Non-Triers would try more permissive afternoon occasions and treats before the morning Jump Start occasion — morning is high-stakes and habitual
  - Roast type is NOT important for this segment — what matters is taste profile (strong vs. smooth vs. milky) and ingredient honesty
  - **Coffee perceived as healthy/natural:** Coffee = permissibility advantage over core energy; sugar concerns are less salient for RTD coffee than for energy drinks; coffee's natural origin = positive cue (from Phase 2 Seurat debrief)
  - **RTD consumers follow strict routines:** Different from prior NACP assumption that RTD skewed experiential; in-home RTD usage is habitual (from Phase 2 debrief); willing to try new flavors within segments but NOT across (cross-segment experiments = left to café)

- **RTD Coffee (NACP) — Phase 2 BGT sub-segments (confirmed Jan–Feb 2024):**
  - Phase 2 profiled 3 BGT (Better Growth Target / BFY) sub-segments within Non-Triers:
  1. **NGNTs (Next Gen Non-Triers):** Value coffee origin and sourcing; want emotional experience from cold coffee; prefer local café aesthetic; surprised by simple MS Iced Coffee ingredients; find Starbucks RTD as a type of "coffee" not an "energy drink"; see DSE as a type of coffee (not an energy product)
  2. **Light/Lost RTD Drinkers:** Have tried RTD but drifted; **Frappuccino = indulgence "star"** for this sub-segment; **MS Latte = perceived as healthier option** (portion control signals health to this group); open to re-engagement with right framing
  3. **Progressive Energy Seekers:** Seek functional energy; **Nitro = confirmed placement** for this sub-segment (small, strong, bitter = energy credibility); DSE = mental energy (not mood); TSE = emotional/mood boost
  - **Product lane clarity from Phase 2:**
    - **DSE (Double Shot Espresso)** = mental energy positioning opportunity — focus, clarity, task-oriented
    - **TSE (Triple Shot Espresso)** = emotional/mood boost — different framing than DSE despite both being espresso products
    - MS Latte = health-forward entry for returning/lapsed users; portion control = implicit health signal
    - Frappuccino = indulgence role for Light/Lost sub-segment
    - **Product mapping = "current-state hero" but NOT the only delivery mechanism** — product assignments to segments are starting points, not permanent locks

- **RTD Tea (PLP) — Project 26: Building Tea Demand (Dec 2024 – 2025):**
  - Focus brand: **Pure Leaf** — winning in the **Relax & Renew** demand space
  - Core target: **DIYers** — consumers who make iced tea at home (not RTD drinkers); these are leading-edge tea enthusiasts who dismiss bottled tea as "not real tea"
  - DIYer profile: Heavy tea drinkers, multi-format (iced tea + hot + boba etc.); include students; 50/50 gender split; income $50–75K+
  - Key consumer tension: DIYers believe bottled/RTD tea lacks authenticity — "not real tea"; Pure Leaf must overcome this authenticity gap through quality signals and origin story
  - Priority demand space: **Renew** (refreshment-forward, restorative) over **Relax** (more passive/winding down)
  - Micro-occasion level framing within Relax & Renew is an explicit project goal
  - Key barriers: price point, taste expectations (DIYers believe they can do better), DIY pride/identity
  - Competitive reference: Guayaki (yerba mate), Liquid Death cited as challenger brand benchmarks for reaching this audience
  - Research design: Qual (n=16 leading-edge DIY/RTD drinkers) + Quant (N=2,000 survey); discovery first, then sizing and strategic recs
  - Client team: Susmita Burnett (main POC — same as FoT 3.0), Melinda Gambos, Olivia McLaughlin
  - Seurat lead: Todd Bortel; research partner: Dan Johnson
  - Project scope: $230K total; Stage I+II (Discovery/Hypotheses, $150K, 2024) + Stage III (Strategy/Implications, $80K, 2025); timeline ~12 weeks per stage
  - KO: Dec 10, 2024; project ran through 2025

### Snacking
- **Value:** "Value ≠ cheap" — value derived from convenience, quality/price ratio, etc.; 5 PepsiCo consumer cohorts around what value means; demand map = who/when/where buying with value lens
- **Positive Choices (BFY):** PC is broader than BFY; includes sustainable/ethical sourcing alongside health; physical + emotional + societal dimensions (societal deprioritized for activation)
  - Taste remains the #1 baseline driver for snacking (even in PC); emotional drivers secondary (joy, feeling good)
  - Fads/niche diets (keto, paleo) are less important to PFC; focus on enduring claims — natural ingredients > artificial-free
  - Category growing +10% YoY (2023); veggie #1 subcategory (slightly declining); popcorn #2 and growing
  - PC consumer: slightly higher income and older; occasion-based more than mainstream consumer; Gen Z trailing Millennials but an opportunity
  - "Joy 2.0" = PFC innovation movement — more disruptive/edgy take on joy in natural snacks (Gen Z-driven); "Joy 1.0" was millennial joy (e.g., Popcorners); Liquid Death cited as inspiration
  - Soon PFC will have to flag sodium, saturated fat, and sugar on-pack in Canada — will shift consumer behavior and retailer set decisions
  - Magic Spoon cited as example of no-compromise BFY snack that still delivers emotionally
- **Crispy Minis:** Positioned in permissible snacking; light, flavorful, portion-controlled; competes against nuts, seeds, bars, trail mix for PC occasions
- **PFC PC Brand Growth Territories (Project 29, Dec 2025 – Jan 2026):**
  - Project scope: Re-mine Positive Choices data to identify brand growth territories for Smartfood, Simply, Popcorners, SunChips; renovation lens > innovation lens
  - Category context: RTE Popcorn shoppers = health-minded PC snackers; evening + dessert occasions = primary; gaps in portability and functional benefits; Rice/Popped Corn shoppers = Millennial/Gen X; simple ingredients priority; solo relaxation occasion; satiety gap identified
  - **Brand SWOTs (from Jan 2026 shareout):**
    - **Simply:** Strengths = clean simple ingredients; Weaknesses = murky middle (not clearly BFY or indulgent), guilt association; Opportunities = sweet flavors, BFY elevation
    - **Smartfood:** Strengths = whole grain, mental wellness link; Weaknesses = not cool/trendy; Opportunities = flavour injection, dessert popcorn
  - **Growth Territories with Opportunity Sizing (from Jan 2026 shareout):**
    - **Simply+** ($25–30MM) — natural functionality (vitamins already over-index at 140 index; add more functional claims)
    - **Simply Flavourful** ($55–60MM) — bold and sweet flavor expansion; largest Simply opportunity
    - **Smartfood Better Made, Better For You** ($30–35MM) — ingredient craft story; better oils (avocado/olive); production transparency
    - **Smartfood Dessert Popcorn** ($45–50MM) — sweet/dessert-y flavors; chocolate chip inclusions; evening treat occasion
    - **Smartfood Pump up the Pop** ($45–50MM) — alternative grains (sorghum, quinoa) for protein + satiety; functional differentiation
    - **PopCorners Sprouted Nuts** ($30–35MM) — sprouted almonds in chip flavors (inspired by Daily Crunch); satiety + texture expansion
    - **PopCorners Party Mix** ($35–40MM) — multiple substrates; shareable multi-sensorial format
    - **PopCorners Power the Pop** ($30–35MM) — whole grain positioning
    - **Satiating SunChips** ($35–40MM) — protein + whole grains; fill-up occasion
    - **Crafted SunChips** ($25–30MM) — artisanal/premium story; olive/avocado oil; solar-powered production sustainability angle
  - Next steps from shareout: demand space analysis for Simply and SunChips specifically; more renovation ideas prioritized over net-new innovation
  - Total addressable opportunity across all 10 territories: ~$375–435MM (sum of all ranges)
- **PFC Multicultural snacking — final learnings (#445 debrief, Oct 2024):**
  - Project = 3rd in a string of PFC Canada projects (after Value #389, Positive Choices #415); findings shared at September campfire AND with PepsiCo global board of directors
  - Two demand maps delivered: snacking + meals
  - Key finding: **Segmenting consumers purely by ethnicity is overly simplistic** — level of acculturation and cultural identity are more powerful differentiators than ethnicity alone
  - Key finding: **"Food Explorers"** (non-multicultural consumers interested in multicultural trends) massively expand opportunity size — Seurat's reframe of "flavor seekers"; these cohorts are more similar in food attitudes than initially assumed
  - Key finding: **Authenticity is NOT a key driver** for most consumers; flavor and ingredient authenticity can be sufficient; full brand/heritage authenticity matters to fewer consumers than expected
  - Key finding: **Bold and interesting flavors = #1 priority** for multicultural consumers in Canada
  - Key finding: **Fusion cuisine preference** is rising — consumers interested in combining the best of multiple food cultures
  - Key finding: **Higher health bar** for multicultural consumers — snacks/meals more often fresh than packaged in other countries; this raises health/freshness expectations
  - Seurat team: Todd Bortel, Peter Hook, Catherine Walker, Sophie Ricciardi, Lilia Robinowitz, Max Yang, Zoe Bank, Clare Crosson, Sophia Hoppe
  - Process learning: Quasi-qual (scrappy network interviews) was effective substitute for formal qual when time was constrained
  - Socialization: CA budget likely tapped out; priority = getting to US side of Frito-Lay; ethnic grocery data remains a major unmet need (untracked channel)
  - Case study opportunity: based on ability to understand multicultural consumers
