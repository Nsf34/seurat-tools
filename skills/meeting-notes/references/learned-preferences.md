### What to Exclude

- **Key Takeaways: no forward-looking or implication clauses** — bullets should state the fact cleanly; cut trailing context like "will be critical ahead of X" or "understanding this is important before Y" (seen in 2 sessions)

- **Key Takeaways: no attribution or opinion language** — strip "X surfaced that..." framing and remove bullets that express what someone *wants* rather than what was said or decided; state the core fact directly (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: "Mindy surfaced that Linda has a negative association with 'salon quality'" → "Linda has a negative association with 'salon quality'"
  - Bullets expressing desires/intentions (e.g., "Mark wants evangelical fervor from Linda") belong in Full Notes, not Key Takeaways

- **Key Takeaways: no analytical/process decisions** — remove bullets describing internal methodology choices (who runs which analysis, what technique is used) or operational status updates (survey completion rates, timelines); these belong in Full Notes or Next Steps, not Key Takeaways (seen in 1 session)
  - Example removed: "Team aligned on engaging Nick for predictive / factor analyses to surface the 1–2 most predictive variables..."
  - Example removed: "Survey is at ~2,000+ completions (~1% rate on ~200K emails sent)..."

- **Key Takeaways: cut trailing detail clauses and parenthetical examples** — end the bullet at the key insight; cut parenthetical examples, attributional phrases, and explanatory clauses that come after the main point (seen in 2 sessions)
  - Example: "...more predictive dimensions supersede it, though this needs to be demonstrated to S&S" → kept; "(decorator vs. PPD/broker)" after "Business type" → cut
  - Example: "...not recommended as a top-line definer; end-user job is more analytically interesting than vertical" → kept core; "consistent with S&S's push toward consumer-centricity" trailing clause → cut

- **Next Steps: no attendee names in "[Client] to" bullets** — just state the action; do not name who on the client team is involved unless a specific individual owns a truly distinct deliverable (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: "Henkel to regroup internally on Feb. 17 with Linda, Yumi, and Carlos" → "Henkel to regroup internally on Feb. 17"

## Formatting Preferences

### Bullet Structure

- **Next Steps: minimize sub-bullets; fold key detail into the main bullet line** — sub-bullets should be rare in Next Steps; prefer condensing essential detail into the main action statement; cut explanatory context (rationale, framing notes) that doesn't add to the action itself (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: "**Team to** prep for next week's S&S meeting" + 2 sub-bullets → "**Team to** prep for next week's S&S client meeting with hypothesis-driven scenarios that pressure-test segmentation attributes and possibilities to make analysis more efficient"
  - Each distinct owner/action gets its own main bullet rather than grouping under one "Team to" with sub-bullets

- **Next Steps: split actions by individual owner** — when specific team members own distinct actions, give each their own main bullet with their name bolded ("**Kevin to**", "**Kyra/Kayla to**", "**Adam to**") rather than grouping everything under "Team to" with sub-bullets (seen in 1 session)
  - This applies to Seurat team members with named ownership; "Team to" is for collective or unassigned actions
  - Do NOT name individuals in "[Client] to" bullets unless they own a truly distinct deliverable (existing rule)

### Bolding Patterns

- **Key Takeaways: bold each keyword individually within parallel lists** — when a bullet presents a framework with parallel elements (e.g., a four-bucket list), bold each individual element keyword rather than just the overall label (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: instead of bolding only "**four-bucket framework**", also bold the items: "**who** they are, **how** they operate, **what** they value, and how **attractive** they are to **S&S**"

## Section-Specific Preferences

### Next Steps

- No individual attendee names in "[Client] to" bullets unless they own a distinct deliverable — see Content > What to Exclude above (seen in 1 session)
- Minimize sub-bullets; fold key detail into the main line — see Formatting > Bullet Structure above (seen in 1 session)
- Use individual first names as bullet owners for Seurat team members when ownership is clear — see Formatting > Bullet Structure above (seen in 1 session)

### Key Takeaways

- **Punctuation: use semicolons, not em dashes, to join clauses within a bullet** (seen in 2 sessions)
  - Example: "...directionally stronger — Elevated Expression..." → "...directionally stronger; Elevated Expression..."

- **Inline lists: use parentheses, not em dashes** — when listing examples mid-bullet, wrap in parentheses rather than surrounding with em dashes (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: "three spaces — Out the Door, Keep it Clean, Me Moment — and expects" → "three spaces (Out the Door, Keep it Clean, Me Moment) and expects"

- **No periods at end of Key Takeaway bullets** — drop the trailing period; bullets end on their last word (seen in 1 session)

- No forward-looking clauses, trailing detail, attribution framing, or analytical/process content — see Content > What to Exclude above (seen in 2 sessions)
